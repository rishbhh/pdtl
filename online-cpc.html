WHAT IS A CAT C1 VEHICLE?

A van or truck that has a gross vehicle weight between 3500kg and 7500kg. Examples include ambulances, parcel delivery trucks, horsebox lorries and small trucks.

YOUR STEP BY STEP GUIDE TO QUALIFICATION

Pre On road training
  • Medical – D4 DVLA form – We can help you with this
  • Provisional – D2 DVLA form – If needed we can help you complete this
  • Start theory training (we provide you with an online theory learning portal) so that you can be learning while awaiting your provisional licence
  • Theory Test – Booked by PDTL or by yourselves at your local test centre (not all Test Centres can carry out LGV) We can pre-book these to speed this timeline up, this can speed up the process by upto 12 weeks
  • NOTE: The theory test is made up of two parts
  • Mod 1 – Multiple choice and Hazard perception
  • Mod 2 – Case Studies – CPC Part 1
  • Book your training and off/on road test with PDTL
On Road Training
  • Training commences – This is normally 10 weeks after the above stage 1 has commenced, could be longer if there any unforeseen complications
  • Mod 4 Training and test – This is carried out at our third party partners site – NOT at the DVSA site
  • Mod 3a – Off road reverse – This is carried out at our third party partners site – NOT at the DVSA site
  • Mod 3b – Lorry on road driving test carried out at the DVSA site
Success
  • Licence received back from the DVLA – usually within 5-7 working days
  • ● Once passed – we can help you on your path to your new career

ABOUT THE COURSE

The Category C or CE driving test is a lorry driving test. To be eligible to take this test you must take the following steps.

1. Book an appointment for a drivers medical either through us or with you own doctor.

2. Apply to the DVLA for your provisional C licence, the process for this, is to submit the relevant completed forms, D4 and D2, to the DVLA enclosing your current driving licence. It will normally take up to 12 weeks currently (usually sooner) for your new licence with provisional C category to return. Contact us once you receive it and you can visit us for a free assessment. This will give you the opportunity to see what we have to offer and we can suggest the correct duration of driver training to suit your ability. If you cannot visit us for an assessment, you could book an ‘average’ length course.

3. Book your theory tests online at www.gov.uk/book-theory-test, you will need to take – LGV Theory Test & LGV Hazard Perception, and if you intend to drive for ‘hire and reward’ you will need to take the LGV Case Studies if you passed your car driving test after 9/9/2009, contact us for advice if needed. You can either buy the Books and DVD’s from us to revise to pass the theory tests or sign up withwww.drivingtheory4all.co.uk, this can be achieved/accessed through this website on the Theory Training Tab, please contact us for further details.

4. Once you have passed your theory tests then contact us to book your driver training course as suggested by the driving assessment.

5. Mod 3a – Off road reverse – This is carried out at our third party partners site – NOT at the DVSA site

6. Mod 3b – Lorry on road driving test carried out at the DVSA site. The driving test will last approximately 60 minutes, then drive onto the roads during which you will complete uphill, downhill and angled starts, together with moving away from the side of road in traffic.
